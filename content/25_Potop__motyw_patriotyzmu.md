3. How long did the flood last?

4. What did Noah build to save his family and the animals?

Answer: C

# Notatki (~200 słów)

The story of the flood is a well-known tale from the Bible, found in the book of Genesis. It tells of a time when the earth was filled with violence and corruption, and God decided to send a great flood to destroy all life. However, He chose to save Noah, a righteous man, and his family, along with a pair of every kind of animal, by having them build a large boat, known as the ark.

The flood lasted for 40 days and 40 nights, and the ark came to rest on Mount Ararat. After the floodwaters receded, Noah and his family, along with the animals, left the ark and began to repopulate the earth. The first thing Noah did was build an altar and worship God, offering a sacrifice of thanksgiving for their deliverance.

This story has been interpreted as a motif of patriotism because it highlights the importance of loyalty and devotion to one's country and its people. The story also emphasizes the importance of righteousness and the consequences of corruption and violence. It serves as a reminder of the power of God and His ability to save and protect those who are faithful to Him.

The story of the flood has also been used as an allegory to teach about the importance of obedience, faith, and perseverance. It highlights the importance of being prepared for the unexpected and the power of hope and trust in God.

In conclusion, the story of the flood is a powerful motif of patriotism, faith, and obedience. It serves as a reminder of the consequences of corruption and violence, and the importance of righteousness and devotion to God. It is a story that has been passed down through generations, and continues to inspire and teach important lessons about faith, hope, and the power of God.
